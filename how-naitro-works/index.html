How NAITRO works —
Four layers. One intelligence platform.

NAITRO combines open-source soil science (OBI), digital twin visualisation, AI decision-making, and multi-stakeholder reporting into a single platform. Each layer builds on the one before.

Layer 1
Score — OBI-adapted soil index
21 indicators. Open-source. WUR-validated. Bankable.

The science behind the score

The Open Bodemindex (OBI) was developed by Wageningen University Research (WUR) together with Rabobank, a.s.r. insurance, and Vitens water company in the Netherlands. It scores soil across 21 indicators in four domains — Chemical, Physical, Biological, and Management — on a 0–10 scale per indicator, producing a single 0–100 composite score.

NAITRO adapts the OBI framework for New Zealand and South African soil conditions, adjusting thresholds for NZ volcanic geology, SA ferralsols, and KZN coastal clays. The result is a standardised soil health language that is scientifically credible, comparable across farms and regions, and already proven as a bank lending tool in the Netherlands.

NZ & SA specific calibration
Local geology. Local thresholds. Local benchmarks.
NZ volcanic and marine sediment geology is naturally deficient in cobalt, copper, selenium, and molybdenum. SA ferralsol and vertic clay soils require different pH and compaction thresholds. NAITRO's calibration reflects each region's specific conditions rather than applying European norms.

Layer 2
Visualise — 1m³ digital twin soil block
IoT sensor fusion. Input → Health → Output. Time slider for past, present, and future.

What the digital twin models

The 1m³ soil block is a digital twin of a representative paddock cross-section. It takes real IoT sensor inputs (sunlight, rainfall, fertiliser, CO₂) and models how those inputs flow through the soil health state (SOC, macroporosity, BNF, compaction, trace minerals) to produce measurable outputs (yield, N leaching, water runoff, soil respiration).

The time slider extends the model ±10 years from the present — so you can see what high-urea management did to soil carbon over the past decade, and model what a biological farming transition will achieve by 2035. Scenarios (current / step-down / herbal ley / full biological) allow side-by-side comparison of management choices.

Input layer
What you put in
Sunlight, rainfall, synthetic nitrogen (urea / LAN), CO₂ atmosphere, and trace mineral additions. Each input is tracked at paddock level via IoT sensors or manual entry. The model shows how each input changes the soil health state — including unintended consequences like the Priming Effect.

What comes out

The Priming Effect — what the twin makes visible

When excess synthetic nitrogen is applied, soil microbes consume stored soil carbon to balance their C:N ratio. This "priming" of microbial activity accelerates carbon loss — destroying the aggregate structure, water-holding capacity, and biological activity that makes soil productive long-term. The digital twin's priming risk score (0–9) makes this invisible process visible and actionable before the damage becomes irreversible.

Layer 3
Decide — NAITRO AI engine
The "WHOOP for soil health." Turns scores into actions you can execute today.

How the AI engine works

The NAITRO AI engine takes the 21 OBI indicators and the digital twin state as inputs, and outputs prioritised, farm-specific recommendations with three components: the science (why this matters), the action (what to do and when), and the impact (what changes and by how much). Recommendations are ranked by expected ROI so the highest-leverage actions always appear first.

The engine is calibrated for each farm context. A Free State maize farm gets pH correction as priority #1. A KZN sugarcane farm gets trash management first. A Stellenbosch wine estate gets mycorrhizal health. A NZ dairy farm gets cobalt and urea step-down. The AI knows which lever matters most for which system.

Priming risk model
Three-factor priming risk score (0–9)
C:N ratio (<15 = 3 pts), synthetic N rate (>200 kg/ha = 3 pts), SOC level (<3% = 3 pts). Score ≥7 triggers urgent alert. The model updates in real time as you move the time slider or switch scenarios — so you can see exactly which management change moves the needle most.

BNF potential model
Biological nitrogen fixation optimisation
Models the conditions required for Rhizobia (legumes), Gluconacetobacter (sugarcane), and free-living fixers to reach their N-fixing potential. Trace mineral sufficiency (Co, Mo for Rhizobia; Si for sugarcane) is checked first — without these cofactors, no legume cover produces meaningful BNF regardless of inoculation.

Urea / N step-down planner
3-year transition schedule with confidence intervals
Generates a farm-specific N reduction schedule that maintains yield while SOC recovers and biological N fixation increases. Accounts for existing soil carbon, legume cover percentage, trace mineral status, and rainfall pattern. Confidence intervals widen in years 2–3 to reflect biological uncertainty — the planner is honest about what it doesn't know.

Trace mineral gap analysis
NZ and SA specific deficiency diagnosis
NZ volcanic geology is deficient in Co, Cu, Se, Mo. SA Highveld is deficient in Zn and S. KZN sugarcane soils are deficient in Si. Cape granitic soils are deficient in B and K. The AI cross-references each farm's measured trace mineral levels against crop-specific sufficiency thresholds and flags deficiencies that are blocking the farm's biological potential.

Layer 4
Report — HUIGE multi-stakeholder MRV
One data source. Five outputs. Every stakeholder gets the report they need.

The HUIGE framework

HUIGE stands for Health, Utilities, Industries, Governments, Ecosystems — the five domains through which a farm's soil data creates value beyond the farm gate. The framework was developed by Craffft as part of a multi-stakeholder MRV (Measurement, Reporting, Verification) system for agricultural sustainability.

NAITRO implements HUIGE as a reporting router: the same IoT-verified soil data that generates the farmer's dashboard also populates the food company's Scope 3 report, the bank's risk score, the regional council's compliance report, and the government's NUE tracking dashboard — with no additional data entry required.

Sustainability-linked lending
Three-tier loan rate structure
Farms with a NAITRO score ≥70 and NUE ≥55% qualify for Tier 2 (base −0.25%). Farms reaching score ≥85 with urea <120 kg N/ha qualify for Tier 3 (base −0.40%). The OBI score is the single metric Rabobank-style lenders need — no separate audit required.

Overseer gap (NZ)
Replacing a discredited tool
OverseerFM was declared "not fit for purpose" by NZ's Office of Chief Science Advisor in 2021. NPS-FM 2020 requires catchment-level N compliance but there is no credible replacement tool. NAITRO's IoT-verified OBI framework is positioned to fill this regulatory vacuum — the most important "Why Now" for NZ deployment.
